avianna

We do original research at the intersection of AI systems, agents, and the future of software work.

At Avianna we study how intelligent systems reason, act, coordinate, and become governable inside real organizations. We work with the executives adopting and scaling AI.

What we study

AI is moving from passive tools to active systems that plan, decide, coordinate, and operate across software, data, and organizations. We focus on the systems layer beneath that shift: agents, workflows, contracts, governance, human delegation, and software architecture.

Research areas

Agent governance

How autonomous systems should be authorized, observed, constrained, and reversed.

AI-native software architecture

How software changes when agents become active participants in workflows.

Enterprise AI adoption

How organizations move from AI demos to durable operating models.

Human–AI collaboration

How people delegate, supervise, evaluate, and coordinate with AI systems.

Mathematical & strategic reasoning

How formal models, systems thinking, and decision theory improve AI and business design.

Applied prototypes

Experimental products and frameworks that turn research ideas into working systems.

For teams building with agents

Concord · semantic contracts for agent-initiated work Project · 3-part series

Concord asks a simple question: when an AI agent takes action, how should an organization know what happened, why it happened, who authorized it, and how to reverse it? Durable execution alone can't answer that. You also need what the action meant, what policy governed it, and what systems it touched.
